About Me:

I work as a C#.NET dev and do game development in Unity3D on the side. I love the constant challenges of game dev, especially when related to simulations, graphics programming, and AI.

I started by making games in VB6 at age 10, and have been using Unity since 2012. Most of my art is custom-made or adapted from sources (SFX and texture libraries, sometimes pre-made models for distant objects). There's always something new to learn, and endless ways to be creative.

In my spare time, I like to read sci fi books, play PC games, rock climb, kayak, paddleboard, skydive, and compose music.

Lazy Eye Therapy Word Search
C#
Unity3D
Android
WebGL
This is a word search game designed to be played with red-blue glasses by people with amblyopia ("lazy eye"). The red and blue elements are only seen by one eye at a time, which trains the brain to use both eyes simultaneously. The UI is intentionally simple.
Features:
  • Customizable 4-tone color palette
  • Settings for game speed, letter size, and screenshake
  • 4 game modes
  • Randomized eye test to track progress
  • Built for Desktop, Android, and WebGL

Wind Shader Collection

Cg
Unity3D
Blender3D
PBR
LOD
Flow maps
Surface shaders for simulating heavy winds on trees and foliage. Used in 'Enchanted Apples'.
Features:
  • Vertex displacement shaders for trees, bushes, and grass at different LODs
  • 3 blend functions: trunk, branch, and leaf
  • Variation created at vertex level by sampling a splat map with object position
  • Optional vertex colors on the models control flexibility
  • Level manager based on Unity ScriptableObjects to control wind direction and intensity
